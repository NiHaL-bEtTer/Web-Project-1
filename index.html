Description

Cricket is bat and ball game between two teams of 11 players.
The game involves playing on an oval-shaped field that has a central pitch where the bowler throws the ball to the batter.
The team batting attempts to score runs by hitting the ball past the boundary and running between the wickets,
but the team fielding attempts to prevent run-scoring and dismiss batters by hitting the batters wickets and other ways.
Cricket has 3 formats, from short T20s to 5 day Test Matches It is particularly popular in India, Australia, England, and Pakistan.

Rules

  • Teams & Players Each team has 11 players, with one team batting and the other fielding.
  • Batting Order Two batsmen are on the field at a time; they take turns facing the bowler.
  • Scoring Runs Runs are scored by running between wickets or hitting boundaries (4 for a ground hit, 6 for clearing the boundary).
  • Overs & Balls A bowler delivers 6 balls per over before another bowler takes over.
  • Wickets The fielding team tries to dismiss batsmen by hitting the stumps, catching the ball, or other methods.
  • Ways to Get Out A batsman can be out by being bowled, caught, LBW (leg before wicket), run out, stumped, or hit-wicket.
  • No Balls & Wides If a bowler oversteps or bowls too high/wide, it's an extra run for the batting team.
  • Innings & Declaring Each team gets one or two innings (depending on the format) to bat until 10 wickets fall or they declare.
  • Fielding Positions Players are strategically placed around the field to stop runs and take catches.
  • Winning the Match The team with the most runs at the end of the game wins.
